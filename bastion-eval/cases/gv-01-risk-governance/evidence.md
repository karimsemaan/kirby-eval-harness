# Enterprise Cybersecurity Risk Management Policy
**Document ID:** MHS-POL-RM-001
**Version:** 1.3
**Last Reviewed:** 2025-11-04
**Owner:** Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) Office, Meridian Health Systems
**Approved By:** Daniel Okafor, VP of Information Technology
**Classification:** Internal

## 1. Purpose
This policy defines how Meridian Health Systems identifies, evaluates, documents, and responds to cybersecurity risk across the organization.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all Meridian Health Systems facilities, including six hospitals and thirty outpatient clinics, and to all systems that store or process electronic protected health information (ePHI) subject to the HIPAA Security Rule.

## 3. Risk Appetite and Tolerance

### 3.1 Risk Appetite Statement
Meridian maintains a risk appetite statement describing the level of cybersecurity risk that leadership is willing to accept in pursuit of clinical and business objectives. Risk tolerance thresholds are derived from this statement and guide all risk acceptance decisions.

> NOTE: A draft risk appetite statement was prepared by the GRC Office in October 2024. As of this revision, **the draft has not been approved by the Executive Risk Committee or the Board, and it has not been communicated to any business unit.** Risk acceptance decisions are currently made case by case with no defined tolerance thresholds.

### 3.2 Risk Acceptance Authority
Department directors may accept risks rated Low or Moderate. Risks rated High require written acceptance by the VP of Information Technology. Risks rated Critical require acceptance by the Executive Risk Committee.

## 4. Risk Assessment Methodology
Each identified risk is evaluated for likelihood and impact and assigned an overall rating of Low, Moderate, High, or Critical.

> OBSERVATION: **No standardized scoring criteria exist for likelihood or impact.** Risk owners assign ratings based on personal judgment. During the Q1 2026 internal review, the same third-party connectivity risk was rated Moderate by one owner and Critical by another, with no documented rationale for either rating.

## 5. Risk Register

### 5.1 Register Maintenance
The GRC Office maintains the enterprise cybersecurity risk register in ServiceNow GRC. Each entry must record:
- a risk description and affected systems or facilities;
- a named risk owner;
- the current likelihood, impact, and overall rating;
- the treatment decision and justification;
- a target remediation date.

Risk owners must review and update their entries at least quarterly.

> OBSERVATION: As of 2026-05-29 the register contains 62 open risks. **47 of the 62 open risks have not been updated in more than twelve months.** Nine entries list a risk owner who left the organization in 2024 or 2025, and eleven risks past their target remediation date carry no revised date and no documented treatment decision.

### 5.2 Risk Response Options
Approved response options are Accept, Mitigate, Transfer, and Avoid. Every response requires a documented justification, and acceptance of any High or Critical risk must be recorded in the register with written sign-off per Section 3.2.

## 6. Reporting and Escalation
The GRC Office prepares a quarterly cybersecurity risk report for the Board Audit and Compliance Committee. The report covers the top ten risks, rating changes since the prior quarter, and remediation status.

An out-of-cycle escalation path to the Committee chair exists for newly identified Critical risks. It was exercised twice in 2025, and both escalations are documented in Committee minutes.

## 7. Integration with Enterprise Risk Management
Meridian's enterprise risk management (ERM) program, administered by the Finance department, tracks clinical, financial, and regulatory risks at the organizational level.

> NOTE: **Cybersecurity risks are not represented in the ERM register, and the GRC Office does not participate in the ERM committee.** The two registers use different rating scales and are not reconciled.

## 8. Compliance
Exceptions to this policy must be approved in writing by the VP of Information Technology and recorded by the GRC Office. Violations may result in disciplinary action.
